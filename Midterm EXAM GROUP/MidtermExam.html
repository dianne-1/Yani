Theory Basics

The Myers-Briggs system describes personality through four opposing preferences. The theory was inspired by the psychological work of Carl Jung and expanded by Katherine Cook Briggs and Isabel Briggs Myers.

Each person falls on one side of four preference pairs. When combined, they create a four-letter personality type such as INFJ or ENTP. There are 16 possible personality types.

Four Personality Preferences

1. Extraversion vs Introversion

  • Extraverts gain energy from people and activity.
  • Introverts gain energy from quiet reflection and solitude.

2. Sensing vs Intuition

  • Sensing types focus on facts and real experiences.
  • Intuitive types focus on ideas, patterns, and possibilities.

3. Thinking vs Feeling

  • Thinking types use logic and objective analysis.
  • Feeling types use emotions and personal values.

4. Judging vs Perceiving

  • Judging types prefer planning and organization.
  • Perceiving types prefer flexibility and spontaneity.

The 16 Personality Types

INFP – The Healer
Imaginative idealists guided by values.
INTJ – The Mastermind
Strategic thinkers who solve complex problems.
INFJ – The Counselor
Insightful individuals who help others grow.
INTP – The Architect
Philosophical innovators interested in theories.
ENFP – The Champion
Creative and enthusiastic motivators.
ENTJ – The Commander
Strong leaders who organize people.
ENTP – The Visionary
Innovative debaters who enjoy ideas.
ENFJ – The Teacher
Charismatic mentors who inspire others.
ISFJ – The Protector
Loyal caretakers devoted to helping.
ISFP – The Composer
Artistic individuals who enjoy creativity.
ISTJ – The Inspector
Responsible and organized planners.
ISTP – The Craftsperson
Hands-on problem solvers.
ESFJ – The Provider
Supportive and caring helpers.
ESFP – The Performer
Energetic entertainers.
ESTJ – The Supervisor
Efficient and structured leaders.
ESTP – The Dynamo
Action-oriented risk takers.
